Graduate Student & Graduate Teaching Assistant

I am a Graduate Teaching Assistant and an enrolled PhD student in the Computer Science department of George Mason University. My research work is mainly focused at the intersection of software engineering and human factors, examining both technical and non-technical influences on human experiences. I engage with practitioners to explore factors that impact their interaction with and effective use of software tools. Currently, I am working under the supervision of Dr. Brittany Johnson-Matthews. Before joining George Mason University in August, 2022, I worked as a Software Engineer under the Software Quality Assurance (SQA) department at Therap BD Ltd. which is a US based company from February, 2020 to July 2022.

I have completed my BSc. in 2020 from University of Dhaka, Bangladesh in Computer Science and Engineering. My undergrad thesis covered a little area of both computer security and HCI. I have also completed my MSc. in 2025 from George Mason University, US in Software Engineering.

Besides, I was a research student at Cognitive Agents and Interaction Lab (CAIL), University of Dhaka. I had been working on a project called "Mind Flare" under this lab and ICT Division of Bangladesh which tried to ensure free and proper psychological support for all.
